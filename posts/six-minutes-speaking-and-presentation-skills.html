Andrew Dlugan from Canada has been maintaining the Six Minutes Blog on presenting for several years . Of all presenting blogs i decided to describe Six Minutes, because I think it is conceptually close to the mindset of scientists - although politicians, news reporters and actors may find it useful as well. The blog covers a wealth of material: The basic toolset of a successful speaker is explained. Lots of articles give tips how to create good PowerPoint slides. Finally, there are in-depth analyses of famous historical speeches that you can enjoy.

If you really have only six minutes to improve your next presentation, my three personal recommendations are:

  • A wise discussion on Guy Kawasakis rule on how many slides to use.

  • A curriculum for beginners: \"12 Days of Ask Six Minutes\".

  • An impressive list of 118 public speaking blogs. Although Six Minutes appears first :-( and two of my pet peeves are missing, that is a lot of information.

However, you probably won't need to read all of them. Your audience will thank you if you try one new technique at a time. Or at least every six minutes.
