A few days ago, I had my debut in Berlin on the first annual meeting of the Netzwerk Wissenschaftsmanagement. I was there to make some contacts. The conference not only featured speakers and participants from all corners of the scientific landscape. Also, the organizers made an effort to diversify the programme, inluding
  • Panel discussions.
  • Special interest group sessions.
  • Five-minute lightning talks.
  • A bulleting board to gather suggestions.
I wish more conferences would be like this one!

Challenges in Science Management

What I learned during the conference is that you cannot put a MBA into a research institution and expect everything to work better. After all, most researchers are an idealistic, freedom-loving group of people, as one speaker acknowledged when talking about staff development:

\"We scientists are not staff and we do not want to be developed. We are special.\"

However, todays' universities need to be competitive, professional, efficient, and transparent. The job of a science manager is thus to create business processes that have the support of all people involved. To communicate decisions in a complex organisation, and to differentiate among particular institutions. And on top of it, we can expect the future to be even more complex. Being a science manager promises to be an exciting job.

Conference Quotes

I picked up some beautiful sentences about managing science, which I translated and paraphrase here:
  • A university is like a garden. It takes time to grow.
  • Before all, make sure you get the right people.
  • Do not let the university become a company.
  • The president of a university is more like a mayor than a CEO.
  • Keep your house in order, whether it is a lab, an institute, or a faculty.
  • As a science manager, you need to have one leg in science, and good friends in the administration.
  • You have to love science.

To which I have nothing to add.
