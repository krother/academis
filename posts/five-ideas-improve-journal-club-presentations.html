I hear very frequently that journal club presentations are perceived as difficult, boring or simply not as good as they could be. I have no ideal recipe to offer, but five things you could try:

  1. Five words
    Ask the presenter to summarize the presentation in five words.

  2. Record
    Switch on a camera, phone or tablet. Share recordings with the presenter only.

  3. Checklist
    Provide presenters with a checklist well in advance.

  4. Feedback
    Share paper slips on which spectators note one positive remark and one suggestion.

  5. Strict time control
    Assign a volunteer to measure time and raise a signal 5' and 2' before the end.

The ideas above can be implemented without a lot of extra effort. There are probably many more things you can try. Keep changing until you find what works for you.
