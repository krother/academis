Giving grades for student presentations is a routine duty in many subjects. As a good teacher, you want these grades to be transparent, objective and just. Most academic teachers are professionals in their own subject, but amateur presenters. The result is that students often end up with grades that are somewhat arbitrary and subjective.

The schema below gives you a scoring guideline for short presentations (up to 10 minutes).Each box below gives one point to a maximum of twenty. The 0-20 points can be transformed to a total score of 0%-100% or whatever grading system you are using.
