Are you losing track of which web tool or social media you can use for what? Would you prefer if your favorite web media would communicate among each other a little more? This is where ifttt comes in. The service allows you to interconnect a multitude of services by defining rules following the scheme if this then that. E.g.:

  • Whenever you write something on Facebook, the same message appears on LinkedIn.
  • Your favorite messages from Twitter get collected on a web page of your choice.
  • When your competitor publishes a paper, you get a text message (via a RSS feed from PubMed).

The services that ifttt can plug together include text messages, weather forecast and even phonecalls. If you want, you can build a complex communication infrastructure - a single event can trigger a chain reaction. For me, ifttt made it a lot easier to distribute my blog announcement to various social media - so I have more time to focus on the content.
