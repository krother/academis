Summary
Your students discover carbohydrates by themselves.
Preparation time
20 minutes
Group size
up to 30
What you need
Prepared A6 cards with carbohydrate structures
Download material as PDF in English
Download material as PDF in German

Problem description

  • What groups of carbohydrates are there?
  • What does saccharose consist of?
  • What does lactose consist of?
  • What differences are there between glucose and galactose?
  • What differences are there between cellulose and amylose?
  • What properties are common to all sugars?

How it is done

  1. Print and cut a deck of 8 carbohydrate cards for each group of students
  2. Explain and show the problem questions
  3. Distribute the prepared decks of cards to groups of 2-3 students.
  4. Give them 20' to work on the tasks.
  5. Collect answers from the entire group, discuss open issues.
  6. Summarize the main concepts (e.g. monosaccharides, disaccharides, polysaccharides, enantiomers, glycosidic bonds).
